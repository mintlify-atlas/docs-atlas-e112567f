## Prerequisites

Before you begin, make sure you have:

- A Courser account (sign up at [chatcourser.com](https://chatcourser.com))
- At least one YouTube lecture video URL
- (Optional) Your own OpenAI API key for higher usage limits

## Create your chatbot

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in to Courser">
    Go to [chatcourser.com](https://chatcourser.com) and sign in with your Google account or email/password.

    <Note>
      Only professors need a Courser account. Students can use the chatbot via a public link without signing in.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new chatbot">
    From your dashboard, click **Create Chatbot**. Give your chatbot a name — for example, "BIOL 201 Assistant".
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your lecture videos">
    Paste one or more YouTube URLs for your lecture recordings. Courser accepts standard YouTube watch URLs:

    ```text
    https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fDAPJ7rvcUw
    https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NJSO52hGZGs
    ```

    <Info>
      Videos must have English captions enabled on YouTube. Auto-generated captions work fine.
    </Info>

    Click **Create** to start processing. Courser will:
    1. Fetch captions for each video
    2. Chunk the transcripts into searchable segments
    3. Generate embeddings and store them in the vector database

    Processing typically takes 30–90 seconds per video depending on length.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Test your chatbot">
    Once processing is complete, you'll be redirected to your chatbot's admin page. Click **Open Chat** to open the public chatbot interface and ask a test question based on your lecture content.

    You should see:
    - A direct answer generated from the lecture material
    - Cited sources with clickable links that jump to the exact timestamp in the video
  </Step>

  <Step title="Share with your students">
    Copy the public chatbot URL from your dashboard and share it with your students. You can:
    - Post the link on Canvas, Blackboard, or any LMS
    - Embed it in a course website using an `<iframe>`
    - Share the direct link via email or syllabus

    See [Embedding Your Chatbot](/professors/embedding-chatbot) for iframe instructions.
  </Step>
</Steps>
